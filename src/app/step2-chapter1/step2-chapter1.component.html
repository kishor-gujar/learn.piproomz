What is Forex?

Forex, or foreign exchange, is the international market on which foreign currencies are bought and sold. This market determines the foreign exchange rate for the currencies that are traded on it. Forex is the most heavily traded, most liquid market in the world by a large margin.The need to exchange one currency for another is a necessity for international businesses and banks. For this reason, large international banks drive most of the trading volume on the Forex market. Retail Forex exists to provide access to the foreign currency markets to individuals, but the trades executed by these accounts make up a mere 5.5% of the foreign exchange market.The Forex market is decentralized, meaning there is no central marketplace. Your Forex trades might be routed through Tokyo, New York or London all depending on what time of day the trade is made. This allows Forex to operate 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

What is a Forex Trader?

A currency trader, also known as a foreign exchange trader or forex trader, is a person who trades, buys and/or sells currencies on the foreign exchange.

Example :

EUR/USD rate represents the number of USD one EUR can buy. If you think the Euro will increase in value against the US Dollar, you buy Euros with US Dollars. If the exchange rate rises, you sell the Euros back, and you cash in your profit. Please keep in mind that forex trading involves a high risk of loss.
